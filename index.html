Exposure Model

How CBAM's full activation and the phase-out of free ETS allowances reshapes cost structures and investment cases across two of Europe's hardest-to-abate industries — steel and cement.

Live policy context: CBAM entered its definitive phase on 1 Jan 2026, with the first certificate price set at €75.36/t for Q1 2026 (European Commission). Free ETS allocations are being phased out to zero by 2034 — reduced 2.5% in 2026, 5% in 2027 (EU ETS Phase 4 Directive). As free allocation disappears, the effective carbon cost borne by European industrial producers rises structurally even if the EUA price stays flat. This model quantifies that trajectory for steel and cement using company-reported FY 2025 Europe segment financials.

⚠ Key assumptions & limitations
Cement revenue per tonne — estimated
Heidelberg Materials does not disclose Europe cement volumes in tonnes. The ~€120/t figure is derived from group cement revenue (€10,967m FY 2025) ÷ estimated ~115mt global volumes × European price premium. Corroborated by IMARC European cement price index (~$144/t, Oct 2025) and IndexBox Germany data ($100–120/t). All other figures are primary-sourced.
Static company structure
Emissions intensity and production routes are held constant at FY 2025 levels. In practice both companies are actively decarbonising — ArcelorMittal's Dunkirk EAF (commissioning 2029) and Heidelberg's Brevik and Padeswood CCS facilities will materially reduce exposure before 2034. The model shows structural pressure without adaptation, quantifying the incentive to invest.
No cost pass-through modelled
The model shows theoretical margin compression before any price response. In practice both companies pass carbon costs through to customers — ArcelorMittal flagged CBAM-driven price recovery from Q2 2026; Heidelberg has strong regional pricing power in cement. Actual margin impact will be lower than modelled.
Methodology & sources
EU ETS & CBAM pricing
Q1 2026 CBAM certificate price: €75.36/t (European Commission, Apr 2026). Free allocation phase-out schedule confirmed from European Parliament legislative record (CBAM Regulation EU 2023/956): both steel and cement follow identical schedule — 2026: 2.5% removed; 2027: 5%; 2028: 10%; 2029: 22.5%; 2030: 48.5%; 2031: 61%; 2032: 73.5%; 2033: 86%; 2034: 100%. Slider maps €75→€200 as proxy for 2026→2034 time trajectory with confirmed non-linear phase-out. EUA forecasts: €85–100/t by 2027. Long-run scenario to €200/t per EY/EC modelling. Heidelberg's own internal planning assumption: €80/t in 2025/26, rising to €100 by 2030 (Heidelberg Materials Capital Market Slides Q1 2026).
Emissions intensities
Steel: 1.8 tCO₂/t (World Steel Association, 2025). Cement: 0.65 tCO₂/t clinker (IEA, 2024); Heidelberg reported 512 kg CO₂/t cementitious material in FY 2025. Free allocation phase-out per EU ETS Phase 4 Directive. Effective cost = EUA price × intensity × (1 − free allocation%).
EBITDA baselines & revenue
Company-reported Europe segment figures, FY 2025: ArcelorMittal steel 7.0% (EBITDA $2,028m ÷ Sales $28,793m; ArcelorMittal FY 2025 Full Year Results, Feb 2026). Heidelberg Materials cement 20.5% (FY 2025 Full Year Results, Feb 2026). Steel avg. selling price: $894/t Europe (ArcelorMittal FY 2025, primary source). Cement avg. revenue: ~€120/t — derived estimate; Heidelberg does not disclose Europe cement volumes in tonnes. Derived from group cement revenue €10,967m ÷ estimated ~115mt global volumes (based on 2022 actuals of 118.8mt with slight 2025 decline) × ~1.25 European price premium.
